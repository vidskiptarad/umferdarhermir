# Speed-limit design requirements for 110+ km/h on divided highways: Iceland vs. Nordic practice

Prepared 2026-07-12. Fact-check of Vegagerðin's claim that no Icelandic road can be posted at 110 km/h because of a required 14–18 m obstacle-free safety zone (öryggissvæði) on each side.

## Bottom line

**Half true and misleading.** Iceland's own design manual requires a clear zone of 10–12 m base width at 110 km/h (reaching ~18 m only after slope add-ons / conservative ministry framing) — and that same manual, like every Nordic standard examined, treats the clear zone as **substitutable by guardrail (vegrið)**. Sweden runs 110 km/h on 2+1 roads with wire-rope medians and clear zones reduced to as little as **5.0 m**; Norway's rule says where the clear zone can't be met, a guardrail *shall* be erected; Denmark posts 130 km/h with an 11 m zone. **"No Icelandic 2+2 road can be 110" is a policy/standard-application choice, not an engineering necessity.**

## 1. Iceland — the actual rule and the actual claim

**Design rule.** Veghönnunarreglur ch. 02 *Þversnið* §2.3 *Öryggissvæði*, Tafla 2.3-1 — minimum safety width A by design speed and ÁDUh:

| ÁDUh | 90 | 100 | **110** | 120 | 130 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0–300 | 9 | 10 | **10** | — | — |
| 301–3 000 | 10 | 11 | **11** | — | — |
| >3 000 | 12 | 12 | **12** | 12 | 12 |

S = A on 1:4-or-flatter slopes; S = A + Δ on embankments (how 12 m grows toward 14–18). Source: https://www.vegagerdin.is/vefur2.nsf/Files/VhRg02_THversnid/$file/VhRg02_Thversnid_2011.pdf

Crucially, the same section: dangerous obstacles within the öryggissvæði **"skal verja með vegriði eða vegriðspúða"** — shall be protected with guardrail or crash cushion (ch. 05.4 Vegrið: https://www.vegagerdin.is/vefur2.nsf/Files/VhRg05.4_Vegrid_2010_a/$file/VhRg05.4_Vegrid_2010_a.pdf). **The Icelandic standard itself allows a barrier in lieu of a fully cleared zone.**

**The actual claim.** Ministry's written answer to Alþingi (þskj. 917/150: https://www.althingi.is/altext/150/s/0917.html; FÍB: https://www.fib.is/is/um-fib/frettir/eng-inn-veg-ur-a-islandi-upp-fyll-ir-skil-yrdi-fyr-ir-110-kmklst): "Til að heimila 110 km/klst. yrði að tryggja að lágmarki 18 m breitt svæði án hindrana." Reykjanesbraut deficiencies cited: narrow zone + light poles on north side; median only half-installed; no alternative cycle route. The 18 m figure exceeds the manual's own 10–12 m base table.

**Legal position.** Umferðarlög nr. 77/2019 §37 (https://www.althingi.is/lagas/nuna/2019077.html): "Ákveða má hærri hraðamörk á vegum, þó eigi hærri en 110 km á klst. ef akstursstefnur eru aðgreindar." **110 is legally available today** — the block is Vegagerðin's standards interpretation, not the statute.

## 2. Norway — Håndbok N101

https://www.vegvesen.no/globalassets/fag/handboker/hb-n101.pdf §2.2: S = A + T-additions; A (Tabell 2.2):

| ÅDT | 70–80 km/t | ≥90 km/t (covers 90/100/110) |
|---|---|---|
| 0–1 500 | 5 m | 6 m |
| 1 500–4 000 | 6 m | 7 m |
| 4 000–12 000 | 7 m | 8 m |
| >12 000 | 8 m | **10 m** (new roads) |

Explicit substitution (verbatim): *"Dersom noen av kravene til sikkert sideterreng ikke oppfylles innenfor sikkerhetssonens bredde (S), skal det settes opp rekkverk."* No separate larger table for 110. Norway has run 110 since 2014 (E18 Vestfold, E6 Østfold, since extended) — median + guardrail, not 18 m open zones.

## 3. Sweden — Trafikverket VGU (directly undercuts the claim)

VGU RÅD 2022:003 (https://trafikverket.diva-portal.org/smash/get/diva2:1621302/FULLTEXT03.pdf) §6.4.2: safe side areas are created by clearing obstacles **"eller så kan sidoområdet förses med skyddsanordning"** — or by fitting a barrier. Alternatives, not requirements.

- **Mötesfri väg VR 110/100** is a named road class incl. 2+1 with wire median (vajerräcke); 2+2 mötesfri / motorväg is VR 110 or VR 120.
- Typical rebuild: 9 m road widened to just **13–14 m** total + median cable barrier (E4 Västerbotten: https://www.trafikverket.se/vara-projekt/alla-strak/e4-motesfri-vag-i-vasterbotten/ · https://sv.wikipedia.org/wiki/2+1-v%C3%A4g).
- Clear zone reducible to **minimum 5.0 m** where full zone/barrier isn't feasible (§7.1.2.4.1, KRAV Fig. 7.8).
- 120 km/h on best low-traffic motorways.

**A 13–14 m Swedish road carries opposing traffic at 110 km/h behind a wire rope — the clearest counterexample to the Icelandic framing.**

## 4. Denmark & Finland

- **Denmark:** 130 km/h standard motorway limit; raising sections to 130 widened the safety zone from 9 m to **11 m** (~+1 m per +10 km/h), barrier substitution applies. (LSF 133 2015/16: https://www.retsinformation.dk/eli/ft/201512L00133)
- **Finland:** 120 km/h summer / 100 winter variable limits on motorways — seasonal sign management, not fixed geometric absolutes.

## 5. Comparison table

| Country | Speed | Road type | Required clear zone | Barrier alternative? | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Iceland | 110 (legal max) | 2+2 divided | A = 10–12 m base; ~18 m ministry framing | **Yes** — "skal verja með vegriði" | None posted; Reykjanesbraut cited as failing |
| Norway | 110 | Motorvei w/ midtrekkverk | A ≤ 10 m + T-additions | **Yes, mandated fallback** | E6 Østfold, E18 Vestfold, E6/E18/E39 |
| Sweden | 110 | Mötesfri väg incl. **2+1 w/ wire barrier** | reducible to **5.0 m** | Yes | E4 Västerbotten etc. |
| Sweden | 120 | High-standard motorway | zone or barrier | Yes | select motorways |
| Denmark | 130 | Motorvej | ~11 m | Yes | upgraded sections |
| Finland | 120 summer | Motorway | zone or barrier, seasonal | Yes | Hki–Turku etc. |

## 6. Conclusion — policy choice, not engineering necessity

No standard examined — including Iceland's own — treats the clear zone as absolute; all offer remove / make yielding / **shield with barrier**. Reykjanesbraut's cited defects (verge width, light poles, incomplete median) are standard guardrail/relocation problems the Nordics routinely solve to reach 110–130. The 18 m the ministry quoted is not even its own design-table minimum. Honest characterization: **110 on an Icelandic divided road is legally permitted (Umferðarlög §37) and engineeringly feasible with barriers; Vegagerðin has made a cautious standards-and-investment decision not to pursue it.**
