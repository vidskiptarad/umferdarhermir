**Scope note:** Chainage (km) figures are approximate — anchored to well-known landmarks; Vegagerðin does not publish a single public per-segment chainage table, so treat distances as ±1–2 km. Points not fully verified are flagged **[UNCERTAIN]**.

**Key status:**
- **Hveragerði–Selfoss ("um Ölfus"):** 2+2 roadbed but **striped/operated as 2+1** with wire median. Down-scoped politically from full 2+2 to "2+1 with option to add 4th lane later." Opened 25 May 2023.

- **Hellisheiði & Kambar:** Hamragil → Kambabrún is 2+1 (wire median); down Kambar 2+2 narrow profile. This was the 2019–2023 widening work.
  Sources: https://www.visir.is/g/20181735801d/ · https://is.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%9Ej%C3%B3%C3%B0vegur_1
- **Under construction 2026:** New Ölfusá bridge; surfacing works near Hveragerði (closures 19 May 2026, 8 July 2026).
- **Net effect:** Rvk → Selfoss now has continuous **separated directions (2+1 or 2+2)** from the capital-area edge to Selfoss — no long 1+1 gap remains, but it is NOT full 2+2.

| Location | Limit |
|---|---|

| Hvalfjarðargöng | 70 km/h |

| Urban approaches | 50–80 |

Iceland's statutory max on rural roads is 90 km/h; no legal 110 category in use.

## Long-range plans

- **Rvk–Borgarnes full 2+2:** No committed plan. Kjalarnes 2+1 is current standard; **Hvalfjarðargöng II** studied (Mannvit/Vegagerðin 2018 comparison: https://wp-beta.vegagerdin.is/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/hvalfjardargong-ii_greinargerd-um-samanburd-gangaleida_april-2018_textinn.pdf) but unfunded.
- **Rvk–Selfoss full 2+2:** Ölfus roadbed is 2+2-ready, 4th lane deferred. No firm date across Hellisheiði. New Ölfusá bridge is the active megaproject.
- **Reykjanesbraut through Hafnarfjörður:** grade-separation Lækjargata–Kaplakriki, construction ~2028.
